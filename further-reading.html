About the Team

Consisting of Graduate students from Indiana University Indianapolis and from Haaga-Helia University of Applied Sciences, Helsinki, our team has come together with one shared goal: to make sense of complex ideas and share them in a way that’s clear, honest, and (hopefully) a little fun.

Ranging from writers to coders to graphic designers and to researchers, we have set out to create an engaging and insightful experience for you to understand the past, present, and future of generative AI and its place in our ever-growing world.

From Indiana University Indianapolis: Amanda Knol, Sarah Ankenbauer, Stormie Franks, Bridget O’ Leary, Noah George.

From Haaga-Helia University: Leo Koivunen, Joonas Lintunen, Selma Essed.

Mission

Our goal is to investigate and examine the changing relationship between artificial intelligence (AI) and copyright laws within the United States, the European Union, and the international stage. Through the discussions and information displayed on this page, our group intends to offer a detailed summary of existing legal structures, current cases, and legislative debates that are influencing the future of intellectual property rights in the AI era. This platform aims to educate Internet users on the intricacies and difficulties involved in safeguarding creative works in the age of AI.
